Garlic Breadsticks Recipe

Description

Breadsticks, also known as grissini, grissino or dipping sticks, are generally pencil-sized sticks of crisp, dry baked bread that originated in Piedmont, a region of Italy. There is also a soft-baked breadstick version popular in North America.

It's believed that breadsticks originated in 1643, when a Florentine abbot described a long-shaped and "bone-thin" bread being made in Lanzo Torinese, a town outside of Turin. Tradition states, however, that it originated in the region of Piedmont in the 17th century, invented by a baker called Antonio Brunero, from Turin. It was a food that was intended to be easier to digest for the Duke Victor Amadeus II of Savoy, who had digestive problems in his childhood.

Ingredients

For The Dough

  • One cup and two tablespoons of warm water
  • One and a half teapsoons of instant yeast
  • Two tablespoons of granulated sugar
  • Three tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted
  • One and three fourths teaspoons of kosher salt
  • Three cups of bread flour or all purpose flour

For brushing

  • Three tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted
  • Half a teaspoon of kosher salt
  • A quarter teaspoon of garlic powder
  • A quarter teaspoon of Italian seasoning

Steps

  1. In the bowl of an electric stand mixer fitted with a dough hook, combine all ingredients for dough except flour. On low speed, gradually add flour. Increase speed to medium and knead dough for seven minutes, or until smooth and elastic.
  2. Remove dough to a lightly oiled bowl and ocver with a dry towel. Let rinse in a warm place until doubled in size, about one and a half hours.
  3. Divide dough into twelve portions. Roll each piece of dough into a seven inch log. Place dough logs on nonstick baking mat or parchment-lined baking sheets, cover, and let rise until doubled in size, about one hour.
  4. Preheat oven to four hundred degrees Farenheit. Bake for about twelve minutes, or until golden brown. Meanwhile, combine butter, salt and garlic powder in a small bowl. Remove breadsticks from oven and immediately brush with butter mixture. Serve warm.
